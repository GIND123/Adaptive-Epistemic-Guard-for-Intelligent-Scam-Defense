AEGIS: Adaptive Epistemic Guard for Intelligent Scam Defense

THE INSIGHT

$12.5 billion was lost to phone scams in the United States in 2024. Every existing detector looks for keywords like "gift card" or "IRS." Scammers adapted long ago. The real attack is not in any specific words. It is in the structure of the conversation itself.

A skilled scammer does not announce that they are stealing from you. They dismantle your capacity to realize it is happening. They manufacture urgency so you react before thinking. They claim false authority so you defer instead of verify. They cut you off from anyone who could help. They plant false premises before you can question them. They flood your emotions until rational analysis fails. They eliminate every exit until compliance feels like the only option.

AEGIS does not detect scam keywords. It detects epistemic manipulation. The difference is what makes it work on novel scam scripts that no keyword detector has ever seen.

WHAT AEGIS MEASURES

AEGIS tracks six manipulation vectors in every conversation, each scored from 0 to 100:

Belief Installation: unverified claims presented as established facts before the listener can question them.
Verification Suppression: active discouragement of independent checking, such as "do not call your bank" or "do not tell anyone."
Urgency Fabrication: artificial time pressure designed to bypass deliberative reasoning.
Authority Hijacking: false claims of governmental, institutional, or professional power.
Emotional Flooding: deliberate emotional overload to disable critical thinking through fear, guilt, or panic.
Exit Path Closure: systematic elimination of the victim's ability to disengage or seek a second opinion.

The Epistemic Integrity Score (EIS) is the inverse of the average vector score. 100 is fully safe. Below 45 is HIGH risk. Below 20 is CRITICAL: terminate the call immediately.

THE MODEL

Base model: Gemma 4 E4B it (unsloth/gemma-4-e4b-it)
Fine tuned with: Unsloth QLoRA on Google Colab A100 (80 GB)
Deployed via: Ollama, running 100% locally on consumer hardware
Model weights: https://huggingface.co/GOVINDFROM/aegis-gemma-4-e4b-it-scam-defense-ollama-gguf-v1

HOW IT WAS TRAINED

I built a dataset of 6,401 synthetic scam conversations spanning six scam types (IRS impersonation, tech support, romance, investment, grandparent emergency, prize or lottery) and 2,839 legitimate call transcripts as negative examples.

440 conversations were annotated with per-vector scores and evidence quotes. I used 396 as the training split and 44 as evaluation.

QLoRA fine-tuning was run with Unsloth using LoRA rank 16, alpha 16, learning rate 2e-4, AdamW 8-bit optimizer, 3 epochs, effective batch size 8. Trainable parameters were 42.4 million out of 8.04 billion (0.53%). Training took approximately 10 minutes on the A100. Final training loss was 0.4065.

After training I merged the LoRA adapter and exported to GGUF Q4_K_M quantization (5.3 GB) using Unsloth's built-in export. The GGUF is registered with Ollama using a custom Modelfile that embeds the full AEGIS system prompt and JSON output schema.

THE APPLICATION

I built a Flask web application with a real-time analysis dashboard:

Left panel: live transcript that builds as turns are added.
Right panel: EIS semicircle gauge, six animated vector bars, risk badge, plain language explanation, recommended action, and verification tool cards.
Bottom: Chart.js timeline showing EIS history across the conversation.

Three input modes are supported:

1. Demo mode: four built-in scenarios (IRS, tech support, romance, grandparent) with auto-play.
2. Live type mode: type any line, assign speaker, analyze instantly.
3. Live audio mode: Web Speech API for microphone input or getDisplayMedia for capturing audio from any browser tab, transcribed locally by faster-whisper (base model, CPU).

The tab audio capture mode allows placing a YouTube scam call video in one half of the screen and AEGIS in the other. Every 6 seconds of audio is transcribed locally and fed to the model. The analysis updates live as the scammer speaks.

PRIVACY

Everything runs on device. The Gemma 4 model runs inside Ollama at localhost. Audio is transcribed by faster-whisper on the local CPU. No call audio, transcript, or analysis ever leaves the machine. Verification tools use hardcoded local knowledge bases with no network calls.

TRACKS TARGETED

Main Track: novel epistemic manipulation detection framework applied to a $12.5 billion annual harm, running on consumer hardware with zero cloud dependency.
Safety and Trust Impact: protects users from one of the most damaging and widespread AI-adjacent harms. The epistemic framing generalizes across all scam types and demographics.
Ollama Special Technology: the entire inference stack runs inside Ollama. No other inference runtime is used. The Modelfile, system prompt, and quantization were designed specifically for local Ollama deployment.
