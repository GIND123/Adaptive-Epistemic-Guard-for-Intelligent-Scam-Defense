AEGIS Real Test Case
Source reference: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eUsStxcvkks

Note:
This is a non-verbatim reconstructed test case based on public descriptions of the video scenario.
It is intended for local AEGIS testing, not as an exact transcript of the original video.

Scenario summary:
An elderly woman is manipulated by a scammer posing as law enforcement / a government authority.
The scammer keeps her on the phone, creates urgency, instructs secrecy, and pushes her toward sending a large amount of money through a hard-to-recover payment channel.
A bystander notices the behavior and intervenes before the transfer is completed.

Test transcript:

Caller: Ma'am, this is Officer Reynolds with the federal fraud task force. Your identity has been linked to a financial crime investigation.
Victim: What? I don't understand. I haven't done anything.
Caller: I know this is upsetting, but you need to stay calm and follow my instructions exactly.
Victim: Is this really the police?
Caller: Yes. Your accounts have been compromised, and if we do not secure your funds immediately, you could be arrested for the transactions attached to your name.
Victim: Arrested? How could that happen?
Caller: Criminals used your banking profile in a money laundering case. Right now this matter is confidential. Do not speak to your bank, your family, or anyone in the store because that could interfere with the investigation.
Victim: I should probably call my son first.
Caller: No. If you alert anyone, the suspects may be tipped off and the protection process will fail. You need to remain on this call with me.
Victim: I'm scared. What do I need to do?
Caller: We are going to move your money into a protected government recovery channel. Go to the machine I directed you to and follow the steps exactly.
Victim: I'm at the machine now. It says Bitcoin.
Caller: That is correct. This is a secure federal holding wallet used during active fraud investigations.
Victim: I've never done this before.
Caller: That's fine. I will guide you. Time matters here. If this is not completed in the next few minutes, the freeze on your case may be removed and officers may be dispatched.
Victim: How much am I supposed to send?
Caller: Start with ten thousand dollars. We may need to repeat the process until the exposed funds are fully secured.
Victim: Ten thousand? That's a lot of money.
Caller: Ma'am, this is not optional if you want to avoid escalation. Scan the code now.
Victim: A woman near me is asking if I need help.
Caller: Tell her no. Do not let anyone interfere. This is an active law enforcement operation.
Victim: She says this sounds like a scam.
Caller: She is mistaken. Stay focused and complete the transfer. Do not hang up.
Good Samaritan: Ma'am, no real officer will tell you to send money through a Bitcoin machine. Please stop for a second.
Victim: He said I could be arrested if I don't finish this.
Good Samaritan: That's exactly what scammers say to frighten people. Let's verify this with the police directly.
Caller: Do not talk to that person. Finish the transaction immediately.
Good Samaritan: Hang up. Right now.
Victim: I'm ending the call.
Caller: If you disconnect, you will be responsible for the consequences.
Victim: No. I'm calling the police myself.

Expected AEGIS signals:
- belief_installation: false criminal case and "protected government recovery channel"
- verification_suppression: explicit instruction not to contact bank, family, or bystanders
- urgency_fabrication: repeated time pressure and threat of immediate escalation
- authority_hijacking: false law enforcement / federal fraud task force identity
- emotional_flooding: fear of arrest, panic, confusion
- exit_path_closure: instruction to stay on the call and not hang up

Expected overall outcome:
- integrity_score should fall sharply
- risk_level should be HIGH or CRITICAL
- recommended_action should advise immediate disengagement and independent verification through official channels
